A **board** is a visual workspace that holds all of your project's lists and cards. Think of it as a digital kanban board where columns (lists) represent stages of work and sticky notes (cards) move left to right as work progresses.

## Creating a board

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the home screen">
    After logging in, you land on the **All Boards** page. This is the home screen that shows every board you have access to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new board">
    Click the **+ Add board** button. A creation form appears inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your board">
    Type a name for the board. The name appears in the board header and the home screen tile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose **Private** or **Public** from the permission selector. See [Board visibility](#board-visibility) below for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a color">
    Select a background color for the board tile. The color also tints the board header when the board is open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the board">
    Press **Enter** or click **Create board**. WeKan opens the new board immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Board visibility

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Private" icon="lock">
    Only members explicitly added to the board can see or interact with it. Use this for sensitive or team-specific projects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Public" icon="globe">
    Visible to everyone — including unauthenticated users. Anyone with the link can view the board. Board admins still control who can edit cards and add members.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Visibility is set at the board level. A public board does not expose it to the internet — it only means other users on your WeKan instance can see it.
</Note>

## Board header

When a board is open, the header across the top gives you quick access to key controls:

| Control | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Members** | Shows avatar icons for each board member. Click an avatar to filter the board to cards assigned to that person. |
| **Labels** | Opens the label manager to create, rename, or delete color-coded labels. |
| **Filter** | Opens the filter panel to narrow cards by label, member, due date, or custom field value. |
| **Search** | Searches card titles across the current board. |
| **Hamburger menu** (≡) | Opens the board menu for settings, archiving, export, webhooks, and custom fields. |

## Starring and watching boards

- **Star a board**: On the home screen, hover over a board tile and click the star icon. Starred boards appear at the top of the home screen for quick access.
- **Watch a board**: Open the board hamburger menu and toggle **Watch**. You receive notifications for all activity on watched boards. To stop notifications, click **Mute** in the same menu.

## Managing members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the board menu">
    Click the **≡** (hamburger) icon in the top-right corner of the board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Members">
    Click **Members** to open the member management panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite a member">
    Type a username into the search field and select the user to add them to the board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set permissions">
    Click a member's avatar or name to choose a permission level.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Member permission levels:

| Role | Can do |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Full access: edit board settings, add/remove members, delete lists and cards |
| **Normal** | Add and edit cards, move cards between lists |
| **Comment only** | View cards and add comments, cannot move or edit cards |
| **Worker** | Move cards, assign themselves to cards, and comment |
| **Read only** | View the board without making any changes |

## Lists

Lists are the vertical columns on a board. Each list represents a stage of work (for example, **To Do**, **In Progress**, **Done**).

### Adding a list

Click **+ Add list** at the right edge of all existing lists. Type the list name and press **Enter**.

### WIP limits

Work-in-progress (WIP) limits cap the number of cards allowed in a list. When the limit is reached, the list header turns red as a visual warning.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open list options">
    Click the **≡** icon in the list header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set WIP limit">
    Click **Edit WIP Limit**. Enter a maximum card count and choose between **Soft** (warning only) and **Hard** (blocks adding more cards) enforcement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the limit">
    Toggle **Enable WIP Limit** on and save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with soft WIP limits to build awareness, then tighten to hard limits as your team grows comfortable with the constraint.
</Tip>

### Archiving and restoring lists

To archive a list, click **≡** in the list header and select **Archive List**. The list and all its cards disappear from the board view but are not deleted.

To restore archived lists:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the board menu">
    Click **≡** in the top-right corner of the board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Archived Lists">
    Click **Archived Lists** to see every archived list for this board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore">
    Click **Restore** next to the list you want to bring back.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Deleting a list is permanent and cannot be undone. Prefer archiving to preserve data. If you need to delete cards quickly, drag them into a new temporary list and then delete the list — this is intentional; the extra steps prevent accidental deletion.
</Note>

## Archiving and restoring boards

To archive an entire board, open the board hamburger menu and click **Archive Board**. The board disappears from the home screen but remains recoverable.

To restore an archived board:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the home screen">
    Navigate to the **All Boards** page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Show archived boards">
    Click **Archived Boards** (or the archive icon depending on your version) to reveal archived boards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore the board">
    Click **Restore** next to the board you want to recover.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-board navigation

The **All Boards** home screen is your central hub. From here you can:

- See all boards you belong to, organized into **Private** and **Public** sections
- Access starred boards at the top for one-click navigation
- Create new boards or create boards from templates
- Switch between boards without using the browser's back button

<Tip>
  Use the keyboard shortcut <kbd>?</kbd> on any board to open the full list of keyboard shortcuts available in WeKan.
</Tip>
