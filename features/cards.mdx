A **card** represents a single unit of work on a board — a task, bug, story, or anything your team tracks. Cards live inside lists and move across them as work progresses.

## Creating a card

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the target list">
    Open the board where you want to create the card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add card">
    Click **+ Add card** at the bottom of the list you want the card to start in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a title">
    Type a title for the card and press **Enter**. The card appears at the bottom of the list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Card titles support Markdown. You can use **bold**, _italic_, or `inline code` directly in the title.
</Tip>

## Card fields

Open a card by clicking on it. The card detail view exposes all of the fields below.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Title" icon="text-cursor">
    The card name. Editable by clicking it directly in the card detail view.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Description" icon="align-left">
    A freeform text area that supports full GitHub-Flavored Markdown, including tables, code blocks, and task lists.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Labels" icon="tag">
    Color-coded labels defined at the board level. Apply multiple labels to a single card for categorization and filtering.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Members" icon="user">
    Assign one or more board members to the card. Assigned members appear as avatar icons on the card miniature.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Due date" icon="calendar">
    A deadline for the card. Overdue cards highlight in red on the board. Date format follows your account language setting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spent time" icon="clock">
    Log hours worked on the card for basic time tracking.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Markdown in descriptions and comments

WeKan uses **GitHub-Flavored Markdown** (GFM) powered by `markdown-it`. You can use the full GFM syntax in card descriptions and comments.

Common examples:

```markdown
## Heading

**Bold text** and _italic text_

- Bullet list item
- Another item

1. Ordered list

`inline code`

> Blockquote
```

Code blocks with syntax highlighting:

````markdown
```python
def greet(name):
    return f"Hello, {name}!"
```
````

<Note>
  To escape an ordered list number so it renders as literal text (e.g., `3. Something`), write `3\. Something` with a backslash before the period.
</Note>

Markdown is also available in:
- Card titles
- Board names (in board view only)
- Comment text

## Checklists

Checklists let you break a card into smaller trackable steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the card">
    Click a card to open the detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a checklist">
    Click **Add Checklist** in the card detail sidebar. Give the checklist a name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items">
    Type an item and press **Enter** to add it. Repeat for each step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check off items">
    Click the checkbox next to an item to mark it complete. The card miniature shows a progress counter (e.g., `2/5`).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can copy a checklist from one card to many others at once. Open the card hamburger menu and select **Copy Checklist Template to Many Cards**.
</Tip>

## Attachments

Cards support file and image attachments.

### Attaching a file

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the card menu">
    Click the **≡** (hamburger) icon on the card miniature or inside the card detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Edit Attachments">
    Click **Edit Attachments** to open the attachment panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an upload method">
    Select **Clipboard** (paste) or **Drag and drop**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload">
    Drag an image or file onto the drop zone, or press <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>V</kbd> to paste an image from your clipboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After the first attachment is added, an **Add Attachment** button appears next to it for subsequent uploads.

<Note>
  Images attached to a card can be set as the card's **cover image**, making them visible on the card miniature on the board.
</Note>

## Comments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the card">
    Click the card to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a comment">
    Scroll to the **Comments** section at the bottom. Click the text field and type your comment. Comments support full Markdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Add Comment** or press <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>Enter</kbd>.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Moving and copying cards

### Move a card

Drag the card miniature from one list to another on the same board. To move a card to a different board:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the card">
    Click the card to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the card menu">
    Click the **≡** icon in the card detail header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Move Card">
    Choose **Move Card**, then pick the target board, swimlane, and list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Copy a card

Follow the same steps as moving, but select **Copy Card** instead. You can also use **multi-selection** to move multiple cards at once: click the checkmark icon on each card miniature to select them, then drag the selection to the target list.

## Card cover images

To set an image as the card cover:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Attach an image">
    Add an image attachment as described above.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set as cover">
    Click the image thumbnail in the card detail view and select **Set as Cover**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The image then appears as a banner at the top of the card miniature on the board, making it easy to identify visually.

## Linked cards

WeKan supports **linked cards** — cards that reference another card or board. A linked card shows the target card's title and acts as a clickable shortcut.

To link a card, open the card hamburger menu and select **Link Card** or **Link Board**, then search for the target.

## Archiving and restoring cards

To archive a card, open the card detail menu and click **Archive**. Archived cards are hidden from the board but not deleted.

To restore an archived card:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the board menu">
    Click the board **≡** icon in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Archived Cards">
    Click **Archived Cards** to see all archived cards for this board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore">
    Click **Restore** next to the card you want to bring back.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Deleting a card is permanent. The archive is the recommended way to remove a card from view while keeping the option to recover it.
</Note>

## Keyboard shortcuts

Press <kbd>?</kbd> on any board to open the keyboard shortcut reference panel. The panel lists all available shortcuts for navigation, card creation, and filtering.

    | <kbd>F</kbd> | Open filter panel |
    | <kbd>Q</kbd> | Filter to your assigned cards |
    | <kbd>Esc</kbd> | Close open panels and dialogs |
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
