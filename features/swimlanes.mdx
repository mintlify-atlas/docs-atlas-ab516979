---
title: "Swimlanes"
description: "Group cards horizontally by team, priority, or any category"
---

**Swimlanes** divide a board into horizontal rows. Each swimlane runs the full width of the board, crossing all lists. Cards inside a swimlane belong to the same row, letting you group work by team, sprint, priority, or any other category without creating separate boards.

<Note>
  Every board has at least one swimlane. When you first create a board and add lists, all cards go into the default swimlane. Swimlanes become most powerful once you add a second row.
</Note>

## Switching to swimlane view

Swimlanes are accessible via the view selector at the top of the board. Click the **Swimlanes** option in the **Swimlanes / Lists / Calendar / Gantt** dropdown to switch into swimlane view.

## Adding a swimlane

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open swimlane view">
    Click the **Lists** button near the top right of the board and switch to the swimlanes view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll to the Add Swimlane button">
    The **Add Swimlane** button appears at the far right of the lists. You may need to scroll horizontally and collapse the right sidebar to see it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the swimlane">
    Type a name for the new swimlane and press **Enter**. The new row appears below the existing swimlanes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Renaming a swimlane

Click directly on the swimlane name (shown on the left side of the row). The name becomes an editable text field. Make your change and press **Enter** to save.

<Tip>
  If the swimlane name is off-screen, scroll all the way to the left — the name label is always anchored to the leftmost edge of the row.
</Tip>

## Reordering swimlanes

Click and hold the name area of the swimlane on the left, then drag it up or down to a new position. Release to drop it in place.

## Moving cards between swimlanes

Drag a card miniature vertically across the swimlane boundary and drop it into the desired row. You can also move the card into a different list within the target swimlane in the same drag operation.

## Default swimlane

The first swimlane on a board is the default. New cards added via **+ Add card** go into the default swimlane unless you explicitly drag them to another row. Rename the default swimlane to reflect its purpose (for example, **Backlog** or **Team A**).

## Archiving a swimlane

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the swimlane controls">
    Scroll all the way to the left to reveal the swimlane header. The hamburger icon (≡) appears directly above the swimlane name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the swimlane menu">
    Click **≡** to open the swimlane options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive">
    Click **Archive Swimlane**. The entire row and all its cards are hidden from view but not deleted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use cases

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Group by team" icon="users">
    Create one swimlane per team. Each team sees their work in a dedicated row while sharing the same list columns (stages) with the rest of the board.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Group by sprint" icon="rotate">
    Add a swimlane for each sprint or iteration. Archive completed sprints to keep the board clean without losing history.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Group by priority" icon="arrow-up">
    Use swimlanes for **Critical**, **High**, **Medium**, and **Low** priority tiers. Cards in the top row get worked on first.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Group by project phase" icon="layers">
    Separate discovery, design, development, and testing into distinct rows so stakeholders can see progress at each phase.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
