---
title: "Custom fields"
description: "Add structured data to cards with custom field types"
---

**Custom fields** let you attach structured, typed data to cards beyond the built-in fields like title, description, and due date. You define fields at the board level and then fill in values on individual cards.

Common uses include tracking ticket IDs, budget figures, priority scores, statuses, or any other data your workflow requires.

## Field types

WeKan supports the following custom field types:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Text" icon="text-cursor-input">
    A freeform single-line text value. Use for short notes, identifiers, or any unstructured string data.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Number" icon="hash">
    A numeric value. Use for counts, scores, or quantities.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Date" icon="calendar">
    A date picker. Use for deadlines, release dates, or other milestones separate from the built-in due date.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dropdown" icon="chevron-down">
    A list of predefined options you choose from. Use for statuses, categories, or any enumerated value.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Checkbox" icon="square-check">
    A true/false toggle. Use for approval flags, blocking indicators, or binary attributes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Currency" icon="dollar-sign">
    A numeric value formatted with a currency code. Use for budget tracking, cost estimates, or pricing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="String template" icon="braces">
    A value assembled from a configurable format string and separator. Use for composite identifiers or formatted references.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Creating a custom field

Custom fields are created at the board level and then applied to individual cards.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the board menu">
    Click the **≡** (hamburger) icon in the top-right corner of the board to open the board menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Custom Fields">
    Click **Custom Fields** in the board menu. The custom fields panel opens on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new field">
    Click **Create Field**. A form appears asking for the field name, type, and optional settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the field">
    Enter a name and select the field type. For **Dropdown** fields, add the list of options. For **Currency** fields, enter the currency code (for example, `USD` or `EUR`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set display options">
    Choose whether to show the field value on card minicards (**Show on card**), always include the field on every card (**Always on card**), or show a sum of numeric values at the top of each list (**Show sum at top of list**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The field is now available to all cards on this board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Applying a custom field to a card

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the card">
    Click the card to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the card menu">
    Click the **≡** icon inside the card detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Edit custom fields">
    Click **Edit custom fields**. A panel lists all custom fields defined on this board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the field to the card">
    Click the custom field name you want to add. It is inserted into the card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a value">
    Click the field value area on the card and enter or select a value. For dropdowns, choose from the predefined options. For dates, use the date picker.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After a value is set, it appears in the card detail view. If **Show on card** is enabled, the field and its value also appear on the card miniature on the board.

## Filtering by custom field values

Once custom fields have values on cards, you can filter the board to show only cards matching specific criteria.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the filter panel">
    Click **Filter** in the board header, or press <kbd>F</kbd>.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a custom field">
    In the filter panel, find the custom field section. Select the field and value you want to filter by.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the filter">
    The board immediately hides cards that do not match. The filter indicator in the header turns active to show a filter is applied.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clear the filter">
    Click **Clear Filter** or press <kbd>Esc</kbd> to remove all active filters.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ticket tracking">
    Add a **Text** custom field named `Ticket ID` and fill in the corresponding ID from your issue tracker. Enable **Show on card** so ticket IDs are visible on minicards without opening each card.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Budget tracking">
    Add a **Currency** custom field named `Cost Estimate`. Enable **Show sum at top of list** to see the total estimated cost per workflow stage at a glance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Approval workflow">
    Add a **Checkbox** custom field named `Approved`. Filter the board to show only approved or unapproved cards during review meetings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Release targeting">
    Add a **Dropdown** custom field named `Target Release` with options like `v1.0`, `v1.1`, and `Backlog`. Filter by release to create a focused view for each milestone.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
